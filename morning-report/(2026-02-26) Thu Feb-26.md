# PJM Morning Commentary — Thu Feb-26

## Commentary

Western Hub DA onpeak collapsed **-$33.33/MWh** from Mon Feb-23 ($76.04) to Wed Feb-25 ($42.71), with Dominion Hub tracking a similar **-$27.20** slide ($68.85 → $41.65) as RTO load shed nearly 10,000 MW over the period. Thu Feb-26 DA cleared marginally firmer — Western Hub at **$44.41** and Dominion Hub at **$41.60** onpeak — finding a floor as wind generation cratered to just **1,118 MW** during peak hours. Wed's DART flipped deeply negative across all hubs, with RT printing **$10-14/MWh above DA**, signaling the market materially under-priced real-time conditions. Fri Feb-27 is the last weekday before Sat Feb-28 and Sun Mar-01 trade flat with no peak premium — expect compressed averages heading into the weekend.

## LMP Price Trends & DART Analysis

**DA onpeak prices fell sharply Mon Feb-23 through Wed Feb-25** — Western Hub dropped from **$76.04 to $42.71** and Dominion Hub from **$68.85 to $41.65**, driven by a $17.37/MWh decline in system energy ($58.13 → $40.76). Thu Feb-26 DA ticked up slightly to $44.41 Western Hub / $41.60 Dominion Hub. **The DA risk premium inverted mid-week** — Mon showed strong positive DARTs (DA > RT) but by Wed, DA was consistently under-pricing RT across the entire footprint.

1. **Wed DART went negative at every hub.** Eastern Hub posted the widest negative onpeak spread at **-$13.67/MWh**, followed by NJ Hub at **-$13.11**, Dominion Hub at **-$10.92**, and Western Hub at **-$10.51**. This is a dramatic reversal from Mon's **+$21.70** at Western Hub and **+$12.36** at Dominion Hub.
2. **System energy drove the inversion.** The onpeak system energy DART was **-$9.81/MWh** on Wed — the entire system cleared ~$10 below RT. On Mon, system energy DART was **+$6.94**, a +$16.75 swing in three days.
3. **ComEd zone DA congestion collapsed in RT.** Chicago Hub DA carried **-$21.76/MWh** onpeak congestion on Tue Feb-24, but the DART congestion component was **+$4.19** — DA massively over-modeled westbound constraints that didn't bind in RT. This pattern persisted through Wed with **+$10.47** congestion DART.
4. **Western Hub congestion compressed.** Western Hub DA onpeak congestion fell from **+$14.90** on Mon to **+$0.45** on Wed and **+$3.52** on Thu. The Western Hub vs Eastern Hub DA spread narrowed from $31.74 on Mon to $7.69 on Thu as the east-west basis differential collapsed.

## Load Analysis

DA **under-forecasted RT** on Wed Feb-25 — DA cleared onpeak at **99,166 MW** but RT instantaneous averaged **~103,100 MW** onpeak, a **-3.8%** error consistent with the negative DART spreads (source: RT instantaneous, metered not yet available for Wed). RTO DA flat load declined steadily from **104,813 MW** Mon to **95,185 MW** Thu, a -9,628 MW drop reflecting late-February weather moderation.

PJM's load forecast for Thu Feb-26 is tracking well through HE9 — forecast **106,919 MW** vs actual **107,200 MW**, a negligible miss. Wed's peak RT load hit **111,434 MW** at HE8 with a steep morning ramp of +13,700 MW from HE4 to HE8; Thu's ramp is tracking similar at +17,270 MW (HE4-HE8). Forecast load for Fri Feb-27 peaks at **109,800 MW** (HE8), then drops sharply to **94,244 MW** on Sat Feb-28 — all hours flat.

## Fuel Mix & Generation Stack

Thermal dispatch held at **70% gas / 30% coal** all week with no fuel-switching signal despite the $17/MWh decline in system energy. Gas onpeak generation fell from **51,930 MW** on Mon to **45,149 MW** on Wed as load dropped, then rebounded to **49,721 MW** on Thu; coal tracked lower at 21,080 → 18,041 → 18,100 MW.

Nuclear ran steady at **~30,150 MW** throughout — no baseload impact. **Wind generation cratered on Thu to 1,118 MW onpeak** vs 5,553 MW Wed and 7,536 MW Tue, forcing ~4,400 MW of additional thermal dispatch and likely supporting the modest DA price recovery. Solar negligible at 127 MW onpeak (early February daylight hours through HE9 only in the data).

## Outages & Supply Availability

RTO total outages rose from **24,355 MW** on Mon Feb-23 to **28,915 MW** on Thu Feb-26, driven by a maintenance surge — maintenance nearly doubled from 7,462 MW to **10,579 MW** as pre-spring shoulder-season work ramps up. Forced outages peaked at **15,401 MW** on Wed but pulled back to **13,910 MW** on Thu — still elevated but just below the ~15,000 MW stress threshold.

Forced outages trended up Mon→Wed (+3,646 MW) before easing Thu — net direction is still bearish for supply. The 7-day outlook shows total outages declining to **24,622 MW** by Wed Mar-04 as forced outages are forecast to fall to ~7,000 MW, offset by planned outages nearly tripling to **12,645 MW** — classic pre-spring maintenance scheduling.

## Tie Flows & Interchange

PJM remained a **net exporter** all week — RTO net interchange averaged **-1,054 MW** on Thu Feb-26, moderating from a peak export of **-3,275 MW** on Wed Feb-25. PJM exported **-2,046 MW** to MISO on Thu (scheduled was only -586 MW), a **-1,460 MW actual vs scheduled deviation** indicating real-time economics strongly favored westbound flow.

NYISO exports held steady at **-1,467 MW** on Thu, consistent with the week's -1,500 to -1,800 MW range. Southern imports strengthened — TVA flows reached **+1,333 MW** and Duke **+811 MW** on Thu, up from +649 MW and +228 MW respectively on Mon, partially offsetting MISO/NYISO exports.
